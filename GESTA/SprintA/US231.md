# JadeWeserPort — Deep-water Container Port (Wilhelmshaven, Germany)

---

## Type of Organization & Ownership Structure
- **Type:** Port infrastructure / container terminal + freight village (logistics hub).
- **Ownership Structure:**
    - Public investment from the German federal states of Lower Saxony and Bremen.
    - Managed/marketed by JadeWeserPort-Realisierungs GmbH & Co. KG and JadeWeserPort-Marketing GmbH.
    - Terminal operations are handled by **EUROGATE**.
    - Shipping companies (e.g., **Hapag-Lloyd**) have recently acquired stakes in terminal operating companies (CTW/RTW).

---

## Activities & Scope of Operations
- **Core activities:** container handling, loading/unloading of Very Large Container Ships (VLCS).
- **Intermodal services:** rail terminal, hinterland connections, logistics facilities in the Freight Village (GVZ).
- **Operations:** 24/7, specialized in deep-sea container traffic.

---

## Main Products / Services & Target Markets
- **Services:** container throughput (TEU), transshipment, intermodal rail transport, warehousing and logistics support.
- **Markets:**
    - International deep-sea routes (e.g., Asia–Europe).
    - Transshipment within the Northern Range (Rotterdam–Antwerp–Hamburg corridor).
    - Hinterland connections to central Europe.

---

## Key Indicators
- **Design capacity:** ~2.7 million TEU per year.
- **Recent traffic (example 2021):** ~713,000 TEU handled (growth compared to earlier years).
- **Infrastructure:**
    - Quay length: 1,725 m.
    - Water depth: >18 m (natural, independent of tides).
    - Rail terminal, storage areas, modern gantry cranes.

---

## Geographic Presence
- **Physical base:** Wilhelmshaven, Lower Saxony, Germany (Jade Bight, North Sea coast).
- **Reach:** international container shipping, connected to major global carriers and central European hinterland.

---

## Recent Developments & Strategic Points
- **Investments:** Hapag-Lloyd acquired shares in the container terminal companies (CTW/RTW).
- **Competitive advantage:** Germany’s **only deep-water container port**, capable of receiving ships with drafts up to ~16.5 m regardless of tides.
- **Focus:** attracting regular liner services, expanding intermodal links.

---

## Challenges
- Under-utilization compared to design capacity in early years.
- Strong competition from other Northern Range ports (Rotterdam, Antwerp, Hamburg).
